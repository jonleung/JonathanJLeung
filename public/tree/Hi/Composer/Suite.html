A Suite for piano is written in memory of my wonderful
music theory teacher, Dr. Andor Kiszely.

I started taking piano lessons from Mrs. Joy Kiszely at the
Main Line Conservatory of Music when I was just 5 years old.

Later, I started taking theory lessons her husband,
Dr. Kiszely, as well.

They were tough teachers, but they were the best.
(It was unusual for students not to play at Carenegie Hall)

I certainly wasn't one of their best students. I would always
spend too much time screwing around on the piano
and not spend enough time practicing.

However, this is what got me started in music composition,
giving me the tools that allow me to compose today.

But even more importantly, Dr. Kiszely taught me to
think logically and to always ask the question, "Why?"

Every day, I realize more and more his impact on me,
and the way that I think.
