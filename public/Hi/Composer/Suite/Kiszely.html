Dr. Kiszely


I started taking piano lessons at the Main Line Conservatory
of Music with Mrs. Joy Kiszely when I was 5 and
music theory lessons with Dr. Andor Kiszely when I was 6.

They were tough, but they were the best.
(It was unusual for students not to play at Carenegie Hall)

I certainly wasn't one of their "best" students: I would always
spend too much time screwing around on the piano
and not spend enough time practicingâ€”
but I guess this is how I first started composing music.

The knowledge of music that I have learned from the Main Line
Conservatory of Music has given me the ability to
interact with music today.

But even more importantly, Dr. Kiszely taught me to
think logically and to always figure out, why things
are the way they are.

I can still remember to this day,
"Why is the G-Cleff called the G-Cleff?"
One of us would raise our hands and say,
"Because it tells us where the 'G' is!",
proud that we knew the answer.

Every day, I realize more and more that his teaching style
has made me into the inquisitive person that I am today.

Today, I am never satisfied until I can answer the question,
why.
